# iSAQB® Advanced Level Curriculum AGENTA

## 2026.1 — Initial Release

This is the first public release of the AGENTA curriculum — a new Advanced Level module for the
Certified Professional for Software Architecture (CPSA®) program. It covers the intersection of
software architecture and AI-driven engineering, with a focus on how architects navigate, shape,
and govern agentic systems.

**Note: This initial release is available in English only.** A German translation is planned for a
future version.

### Curriculum Content

The curriculum is structured into seven learning units that build on each other progressively.
It opens with an introduction to the fundamental concepts of agentic engineering and its
implications for software architecture, then moves into AI-assisted architectural decision-making —
how architects can effectively use AI tools to reason about and document design choices.

The middle chapters address the practical challenges of working with agents: how to provide
architectural knowledge to agents in a form they can use, how to keep agents aligned with
architectural goals over time, and how to extract and interpret architectural information from
AI-generated artifacts and system behavior.

The final two chapters zoom out to the operational and professional dimension. One chapter covers
governance and quality gates for AI usage, including how to establish guardrails within an
architecture organisation. The closing chapter reflects on how the discipline of software
architecture itself is evolving in response to agentic engineering — touching on the shifting role
of the architect and the new skills the discipline demands.
